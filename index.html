Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Obesity
- There is a slightly positive correlation between states' obesity rates and the poverty rate.
- Age and Obesity do not have a meaningful correlation at the state level.
- Obesity and Median Household Income have a clearly negative correlation. This means, obesity rates are lower in states with a higher Median Household Income.

Smoking
- Smoking and Poverty have a visible weak positive correlation.
- When it comes to Smoking and Age, it appears that most states fall within a ten year age range for their median smoker's age. 4 of the datapoints in the entire dataset fall outside of ages 32 to 42.
- Similar to Obesity and Median Household Income, we see a strong negative correlation between the percentange of smokers and the Median Household Income.

Healthcare
- There is a strong positive correlation between the percentange of residents without healthcare and the percentange of residents in poverty.
- Again, the Age group seems to show no correlation, and most datapoints fall between 32 and 42.
- The weakest of the three groups, Median Household Income and percentange of residents lacking healthcare still show a visible negative correlation.
